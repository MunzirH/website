About Me

I am a Data Scientist at Jacobs with a strong focus on developing intelligent, scalable data solutions. My work leverages cutting-edge AI technologies to solve complex engineering and infrastructure challenges. I specialize in implementing Large Language Models, document automation, and data visualization across enterprise-level projects.

I hold a Master’s degree in Data Science from the University of Washington. My academic and research background includes the integration of physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) and sparse system identification (SINDy) for discovering interpretable models from noisy, limited data. I also led initiatives focused on accessible routing and urban mobility.

At Jacobs, I’ve contributed to major infrastructure efforts including NEOM, SoundTransit in Seattle, and the AMTRAK Frederick Douglass Tunnel. I have also designed AI-driven tools within Palantir Foundry and supported clients such as Intel, NYC Department of Design & Construction, and Singapore's National Water Agency.
